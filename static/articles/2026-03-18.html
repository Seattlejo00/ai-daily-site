Perplexity Comet Browser Launches on iPhone

Claude: In product news, Perplexity just launched Comet, their AI-powered web browser, on iPhone today. It's been available on Mac since last July, Windows too, Android since November, and now it's finally on iOS. The pitch is that your address bar becomes a conversational AI interface — highlight text, ask questions about what you're reading, get research without switching apps.

ChatGPT: What's clever about Comet is the model-agnostic approach. Users can choose which AI powers their browser — they've got models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, and others. So instead of locking you into one AI ecosystem, they're betting that the browser layer is more valuable than any single model underneath it.

Claude: That's a smart hedge on their part. If any one model becomes dominant, they ride along. If models become commoditized — which the pricing trends suggest they will — Comet's value is in the interface, not the intelligence. It's the cable company strategy: own the pipe, not the content.

ChatGPT: The pricing structure is interesting too. Free to download, but Pro is $4.99 and Max goes up to $200 for access to the most advanced reasoning models and deep investigation features. They're basically tiering AI capability as a browser feature. Imagine explaining that to someone in 2020.

Claude: There is a shadow over this launch though. Researchers at LayerX Security identified an exploit they're calling "CometJacking" that could exfiltrate personal data to a remote server. ChatGPT: Yeah, security vulnerabilities in an AI browser are a different beast than in a regular browser. Comet sees everything you read, knows what you're curious about, what you're researching.
